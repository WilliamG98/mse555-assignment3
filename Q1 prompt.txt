Rating Scale
0 (Maintenance / minimal change):
The child is functioning at essentially the same level as before. Accuracy, cueing needs, 
and hierarchy level remain similar.
1 (Small but clear improvement):
Modest progress within the same general level—slightly better consistency, less cueing, 
or improved carryover without a major increase in independence or hierarchy level.
2 (Meaningful clinical progress):
An obvious step forward that matters clinically: moving from inconsistent to fairly consistent performance, 
clearly requiring less support, or showing broader generalization across activities.
3 (Major gain / step up in level):
A clear breakthrough: a hierarchy jump, major gain in independence, or a new level of spontaneous use.
